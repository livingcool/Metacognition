# Mirror — User Interview Guide
*Facilitator Document · March 2026*

---

## Pre-Interview Setup

- **Duration**: 45 Minutes.
- **Participant**: Founders, knowledge workers, or creators.
- **Incentive**: Early access to Mirror + Beta status.
- **Goal**: Understand their current decision-making archaeology and reflection habits.

---

## 1. Warm-Up (5 min)

- Briefly introduce yourself and the Mirror project.
- **Mirror Concept**: "A thinking tool that reflects your cognitive patterns back to you using research-grounded science."
- **Current Role**: "What is your main professional context? What kind of decisions do you make daily?"

---

## 2. Decision Archaeology (15 min)

- "Tell me about a high-stakes decision you made recently. Walk me through the timeline."
- "What were the main sources of information you used?"
- "At any point, did you feel a sudden surge of certainty? What caused it?"
- "Looking back, what was the biggest 'unknown' that you treated as a 'known' at the time?" (Searching for *Certainty Surge* or *Overconfidence*).

---

## 3. Reflection Habits (10 min)

- "How do you currently reflect on your decisions after the fact?"
- "What tools do you use? (e.g. journaling, talking to peers, retrospectives)."
- "What is missing from your current reflection process? What do you still feel blind to?"

---

## 4. Concept Validation (10 min)

- "If an AI could identify your dominant cognitive biases (e.g. status quo bias, planning fallacy) and cite the exact research behind it, how would you use that information?"
- "What would make you distrust such a system?"
- "Would you prefer a confrontational 'mirror' or a gentle 'guide'?"

---

## 5. Closing (5 min)

- Ask if they'd like to be part of the Alpha cohort.
- Any final thoughts on their own thinking process?
- Thank them for their time.
