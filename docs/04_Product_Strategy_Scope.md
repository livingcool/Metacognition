## 0. The One-Line Truth

**Mirror exists because every human has a pattern in how they think, almost nobody can see it, and nobody has built a tool that shows it to them.**

---

## 1. The Problem — In Full

### 1.1 The Invisible Thinking Loop

Most people, when they make a bad decision, believe it was a one-off. It wasn't. Cognitive science has established conclusively over sixty years of research — from Kahneman and Tversky's original prospect theory work to more recent meta-analyses on decision-making under uncertainty — that human thinking patterns are highly consistent across time, context, and stakes.

You do not have random bad decisions. You have a *signature* set of cognitive patterns that play out every time the conditions are right. Overconfidence when the timeline is short. Certainty surge when you've committed to a direction. Confirmation bias when the stakes are high. The pattern is yours. It repeats. You can't see it.

The reason you can't see it is structural, not personal:
1. **No memory across decisions.** There is no system that connects your decision in Q1 to your decision in Q3 and says "this is structurally identical."
2. **No grounded feedback.** Even therapists and coaches give feedback based on what they remember, which is what you told them, which is already filtered through your own blind spots.
3. **No research grounding.** Nobody connects your personal pattern to the 60 years of cognitive science literature that has already named and described it precisely.

### 1.2 What Exists Today (and Why It Falls Short)

| Category | Examples | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| Journaling apps | Day One, Notion | Passive storage. No analysis. No pattern surfacing. |
| Therapy / coaching | Human conversation | Expensive, unscalable, practitioner memory is fallible |
| Decision frameworks | WRAP, pre-mortem, 10/10/10 | Useful but one-off. No personalised history. |
| Personality assessments | MBTI, Big Five | Trait-level, static. Not decision-level, not dynamic. |
| AI chatbots | ChatGPT, Claude | No persistent memory. No research grounding. No profile. |
| Meditation apps | Headspace, Calm | Emotional regulation. Not cognitive pattern analysis. |

None of these build a model of *how you specifically think* over time, grounded in research, continuously improving.

### 1.3 The Gap Mirror Fills

```
WHAT EXISTS                          WHAT MIRROR IS

Journaling (no analysis)    <-->     Analysis with research grounding
Coaching (no memory)        <-->     Persistent cognitive profile
```

## 2. The Vision

**Mirror is the world's first personalised metacognition engine — a system that builds a long-term model of how you think, surfaces your cognitive patterns when they appear, and makes you measurably better at thinking over time.**

Not "your AI therapist." Not "a journaling app with AI." Something that has never existed before: a research-grounded, memory-persistent, personalised cognitive mirror.

In ten years, Mirror is the tool that the world's best thinkers — founders, executives, scientists, creators, anyone who makes consequential decisions — use as a daily cognitive instrument. The way people use a gym for the body, they use Mirror for the mind.

## 4. Target Users — Who Mirror Is For

### 4.1 Primary Archetypes

**Archetype 1: The Consequential Maker**
- Founders, executives, senior managers
- Makes 3–5 high-stakes decisions per week
- Has been wrong before in ways that cost them significantly
- Values intellectual honesty; has read Kahneman
- Pain point: "I know I have blind spots. I just can't see them in the moment."

**Archetype 2: The Growth-Oriented Professional**
- Knowledge workers aged 25–40 in high-performance roles
- Uses journaling, therapy, or regular retrospectives
- Actively invests in personal development
- Pain point: "I do the work but I can't see the progress or the pattern."

**Archetype 3: The Coach or Therapist**
- Professional practitioners working with clients on decision-making
- Wants a tool that captures cognitive data between sessions
- Pain point: "I rely on what clients tell me. They filter everything through their own blind spots."

**Archetype 4: The Researcher or Academic**
- Works in knowledge-intensive environments
- Rigorous, sceptical, evidence-hungry
- Will only trust a tool that cites primary sources
- Pain point: "Most productivity tools are pseudoscientific. I need something grounded in actual research."

### 4.2 Who Mirror Is NOT For

- People seeking emotional support or mental health treatment (Mirror is not a clinical tool)
- Casual journalers who want to store memories
- People with no interest in their own thinking patterns
- Users unwilling to engage in the feedback loop (choice cards, predictions, weekly review)

## 5. The Value Proposition — Layer by Layer

### For the User

1. **See your pattern.** Not "you seem stressed" — but "this is certainty surge, defined by Kahneman in 2011, and here is exactly how it is appearing in what you just wrote."

2. **Remember across time.** "Three weeks ago you said something structurally identical. You acted with certainty. It did not work out. What is different now?"

3. **Measure your growth.** "Your calibration score — how often you are right when you say you're certain — has improved from 0.61 to 0.74 over the past six sessions."

4. **Get research, not opinion.** Every pattern Mirror surfaces is tied to a real paper from the metacognition literature. Mirror is not guessing. It is reading your thinking against 200+ papers.

5. **The loop compounds.** The more you use Mirror, the more precise it becomes. Because confirmed choice signals (weight: 0.9) are stronger than inferred patterns. Because the cognitive profile builds depth with every session.

### For the RootedAI Business

1. **Network effects of personal data.** The more sessions a user has, the more valuable their Mirror instance is. Switching costs are enormous after 20 sessions.

2. **Research corpus as moat.** 500+ curated, embedded, quality-controlled metacognition paper chunks are not easy to replicate. The corpus is a genuine competitive advantage.

3. **B2C subscription with B2B expansion.** Individual subscribers are the core; enterprise (teams, coaching practices, leadership programmes) is the natural expansion.

## 8. Product Principles

1. **One question.** Mirror asks exactly one question per turn. Never two. The discipline of a single question is one of Mirror's strongest design decisions — it forces the reasoning engine to find the most important question rather than asking several weak ones.

2. **Research or silence.** Every pattern Mirror surfaces is tied to a real paper from the metacognition literature. Mirror does not surface "hunches." If the pattern cannot be cited, it is not surfaced.

3. **The profile is private.** Mirror will never share, sell, or expose a user's cognitive profile. The profile is the user's. The more explicit and contractual this commitment, the better.

4. **Growth must be measurable.** Mirror ships features only if they make the calibration score, pattern accuracy, or user engagement measurably better. Vibes-based features do not ship.

5. **The loop compounds.** Mirror improves with every session. The experience at session 20 must be meaningfully better than session 1. This is not a goal; it is a functional requirement.

## 9. Success Metrics (North Star)

Mirror's success is measured on one north star metric:

> **% of users whose calibration score improves over 90 days of usage**

This metric captures everything: engagement (they used it enough), quality (Mirror was accurate enough), and value (it actually changed how they think).

Supporting metrics:
- Sessions per user per week (engagement)
- Choice card selection rate (active participation in the loop)
- 90-day retention (long-term value delivery)
- NPS > 50 (strong word-of-mouth potential)
- LLM cost per session < $0.02 (unit economics health)
